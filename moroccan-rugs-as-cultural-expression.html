Moroccan rugs play an important role in daily life, serving not only as decorative pieces but also as functional items in homes. They are often used for seating, sleeping, and social gatherings, making them integral to Moroccan hospitality and communal life.

How do Moroccan rug styles differ from Persian rugs?

While both Moroccan and Persian rugs are highly valued for their craftsmanship, they differ in design, texture, and weaving techniques. Moroccan rugs typically feature abstract patterns and bold colors, while Persian rugs often exhibit intricate floral designs and a more refined color palette.
